New Homeowner Guide

Mason Oaks homes feature beautiful quartz countertops that require periodic sealing to maintain their appearance and durability.

Recommended Schedule:
  • Apply quartz sealer every 12-18 months
  • First application should be within 3 months of move-in
Recommended Products:
  • Stone Care International Quartz Sealer
  • Granite Gold Quartz Brite
How to Apply:
  1. Clean countertops thoroughly and allow to dry completely
  2. Apply sealer in small sections with a clean microfiber cloth
  3. Allow to penetrate for 5-10 minutes (follow product instructions)
  4. Buff with a clean cloth until dry
  5. Avoid using countertops for 24 hours after sealing

Properly sealed grout prevents staining and extends the life of your tile installations in bathrooms and kitchen areas.

Recommended Schedule:
  • Seal bathroom grout within 3 months of move-in
  • Reseal kitchen and bathroom grout annually
Recommended Products:
  • Aqua Mix Sealer's Choice Gold
  • Custom Building Products TileLab Grout Sealer
How to Apply:
  1. Clean grout lines thoroughly with a grout cleaner
  2. Allow to dry completely (24 hours recommended)
  3. Apply sealer with an applicator brush or bottle with applicator tip
  4. Work in small sections, ensuring grout lines are fully saturated
  5. Allow sealer to penetrate for 5-10 minutes
  6. Wipe away excess sealer from tile surfaces
  7. Allow 24-48 hours for sealer to cure before exposing to water

Regular HVAC filter changes improve indoor air quality and help your system operate efficiently.

Recommended Schedule:
  • 1-2 inch filters: Every 1-3 months
  • 4-5 inch filters: Every 6-12 months
  • More frequent changes during high-use seasons or if you have pets
Filter Sizes in Mason Oaks Homes:
  • Most homes use 16x25x1 filters (check your specific unit)
  • Some floor plans may use different sizes
How to Change:
  1. Turn off your HVAC system
  2. Locate the filter compartment (usually near the air handler)
  3. Remove the old filter, noting the direction of airflow arrow
  4. Insert the new filter with the arrow pointing toward the unit
  5. Secure the filter compartment
  6. Turn the system back on

Proper water heater maintenance extends the life of your unit and ensures efficient operation.

Recommended Schedule:
  • Flush water heater annually
  • Check pressure relief valve every 6 months
How to Flush Water Heater:
  1. Turn off power to electric heaters or set gas heaters to "pilot"
  2. Turn off cold water supply
  3. Connect a garden hose to the drain valve
  4. Open a hot water faucet in the house to prevent vacuum
  5. Open the drain valve and allow tank to empty
  6. Turn on cold water supply briefly to flush out sediment
  7. Close drain valve, remove hose, and refill tank
  8. When water flows normally from the open faucet, turn power back on

Note: If you're uncomfortable performing this maintenance yourself, consider hiring a professional plumber.

Air trapped in water lines can cause sputtering faucets and reduced water flow. Bleeding the lines removes these air pockets.

When to Bleed Lines:
  • After moving into a new home
  • After water service interruptions
  • When you hear knocking in pipes
  • When water flow is inconsistent
How to Bleed Air from Lines:
  1. Turn off main water supply
  2. Open the highest faucet in your home
  3. Open the lowest faucet (usually in basement or first floor)
  4. Turn water supply back on slowly
  5. Allow water to run until it flows smoothly without sputtering
  6. Close the lowest faucet first, then the highest

Repeat process for hot water lines by running hot water until it flows smoothly.

Frequently Asked Questions

If you've lost mail, fill out the lost mail form on the USPS website. You can also contact the Wake Forest Post Office at (800) 275-8777 to inquire about missing mail.

HOA dues are collected quarterly and are due on the 1st of January, April, July, and October. Payment options include:

  • Online through the PPM Engage Portal
  • Through the PPM Community Portal mobile app
  • By check mailed to the management company (address available in your welcome packet)
  • Auto-draft (contact PPM to set up)

All exterior modifications require prior approval from the Architectural Review Committee (ARC). To submit a request:

  1. Log in to the PPM Engage Portal
  2. Navigate to the "ARC Requests" section
  3. Complete the form with detailed description and attach any plans, drawings, or product specifications
  4. Submit your request

The ARC typically reviews requests within 30 days. Do not begin work until you receive written approval.

The Mason Oaks community pool is typically open from Memorial Day weekend through Labor Day weekend, weather permitting. Pool hours are:

  • Monday - Saturday: 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM
  • Sunday: 12:00 PM - 8:00 PM

Access requires your community key fob. Pool rules and regulations are posted at the pool area and available on the community portal.

For general community questions or concerns, you have several resources:

  • Property Management: PPM Real Estate - (919) 551-8088 or through the community portal
  • HOA Board: Email board@masonoakshoa.com
  • Facebook Group: Join our community Facebook group to connect with neighbors
  • Community Meetings: Attend quarterly board meetings (dates announced on the community portal)

In Mason Oaks, trash and recycling are collected on Wednesdays. Please follow these guidelines:

  • Place bins at the curb by 6:00 AM on Wednesday
  • Remove bins by 7:00 PM the same day
  • Store bins out of sight from the street when not in use
  • Bulk item pickup requires scheduling with the town

For holiday schedule adjustments or more information, visit the Town of Wake Forest website.
